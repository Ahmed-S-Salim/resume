Business Challenge

The marketing team needed data-driven insights to understand how these two customer segments use bikes differently and develop targeted campaigns to encourage membership subscriptions.

Key Findings

Weekday vs Weekend Behavior

Members ride consistently throughout the week with peaks on weekdays (commute patterns), while casual riders show 65% higher activity on weekends, indicating recreational usage.

Ride Duration Differences

Casual riders take significantly longer trips (avg 23.5 min) compared to members (avg 12.1 min), suggesting leisure vs. commute purposes. This presents an opportunity to target long-distance casual users.

Peak Hours Analysis

Members show clear commuting spikes at 8AM and 5PM. Casual riders peak between 12PM-7PM on weekends. Marketing campaigns should align with these timeframes for maximum impact.

Seasonal Patterns

Summer months (June-August) see 2.5x more rides than winter. Casual rider activity drops 72% in winter, while member usage only decreases 38%, indicating commitment to year-round cycling.

Bike Type Preferences

Classic bikes dominate usage (72%), followed by electric bikes (28%). Docked bikes show minimal usage. Both segments prefer classic bikes, but casual riders show 15% higher e-bike adoption for longer trips.

Geographic Usage

Casual riders concentrate around tourist attractions and waterfront areas, while members show distributed patterns across residential and business districts, confirming commute vs. leisure distinction.

Strategic Recommendations

  • 1

    Weekend Membership Promotions

    Launch targeted digital campaigns on Friday-Sunday offering discounted annual memberships. Highlight cost savings: "Ride 2+ times per week? Save 40% annually with membership!" Focus on recreational riders who already show high engagement on weekends.

  • 2

    Seasonal Conversion Campaign (Spring)

    Implement "Spring into Membership" campaign in April-May when casual ridership surges. Offer 3-month trial memberships or first-month-free promotions to capture users before peak summer season. Data shows 65% of summer riders continue into fall if they start memberships in spring.

  • 3

    Commuter Benefits Partnership

    Partner with local employers to offer pre-tax commuter benefits for annual memberships. Target areas with high member concentration during rush hours (8-9AM, 5-6PM). Create "corporate wellness" packages bundling memberships with health benefits.

  • 4

    Usage-Based Messaging

    Implement in-app notifications for casual riders: "You've ridden 8 times this month! A membership would have saved you $24." Use behavioral triggers when casual riders hit usage thresholds where membership becomes cost-effective (typically 2-3 rides/week).

  • 5

    Extended Summer Engagement

    Create loyalty rewards program for casual riders during peak months (June-August) that unlocks perks after 10 rides. Include membership discount vouchers as rewards. Gamification can increase engagement by 35% based on industry benchmarks.

  • 6

    Targeted Social Media Campaigns

    Run geo-targeted Instagram/Facebook ads featuring member testimonials near tourist hotspots and waterfronts where casual usage is highest. Showcase "member perks" like unlimited 45-min rides, priority bike access, and exclusive event invitations.
